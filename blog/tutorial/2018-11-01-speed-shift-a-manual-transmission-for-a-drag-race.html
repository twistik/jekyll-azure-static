How to shift manual gearbox gears at the level of race car pros and be the fastest on the road

Keep in mind that this can be very hard on your clutch and your manual transmission, and it may cause excessive wear.

Use a dynamometer to determine the correct power band for each gear of your manual transmission. While many believe that the engine delivers the most power just before the redline, the maximum power band actually occurs before that. Most repair garages have a dynamometer and can perform this task for you.

Consider purchasing a mounted tachometer with a shift light indicator that can be adjusted to reflect the maximum power for each gear. Since you’ll be able to see the shift indicator in your peripheral vision, you can safely keep your eyes on the road while still knowing the optimal time to shift.

Implement the speed-shifting technique for your manual transmission by revving the engine before launching, typically between 2000 and 3500 RPM, depending on your vehicle’s torque (the more torque you have, the less you’ll need to rev the engine). Remember, the less you spin your tires unnecessarily, the faster your launch will be.

Try shifting 200 to 300 RPM before you hit the optimal power band for each gear. This will help compensate for the reaction time needed to see the shift indicator, which can take a second or even longer.

The final method described requires precise timing and skill and is known as "power shifting."

Keep your foot on the gas during each gear shift. This will cause your engine to rev to its maximum while the clutch is engaged, so you’ll need to make your transition quickly enough to avoid hitting the redline, which could either burn out your engine or trigger the rev limiter, causing your engine to cut out. In either case, you’ll lose the race!
