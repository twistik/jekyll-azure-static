The BMW R1250GS and BMW R1250RT have been announced by BMW

Cleaner, smarter and more powerful - the BMW R1250GS and BMW R1250RT have just been announced by BMW motorcyclesrrad with a host of technical and mechanical updates. Overall, the updates add power, safety and convenience, bringing 35 years of the boxer into the future.

The big intrigue is that power and torque have been increased for the R1250GS and R1250RT to a claimed 136bhp at 7,250rpm and 105lb-ft of torque 6,250rpm, a gain of 11bhp and 28lb-ft respectively. How this gain is achieved is a mixture of litre and technical improvements. Both models have increased displacement by 84cc to 1,250cc, something we regularly see with other manufacturers, to meet increasingly stringent emissions requirements while maintaining current power levels. In addition, for extra power and lower emissions, BMW has added new ShiftCam technology

ShiftCam-This camshift setting is a first for BMW and features an additional cam profile activated by a set of pins sliding inside to move the camshaft axially. This allows BMW to optimise clean emissions with power smoothness, plus the system adds power in the upper rev range.

Completing the engine changes is a toothed chain to drive the camshaft instead of a roller drive, an ‘optimised’ oil supply, dual jet injection valves and a new exhaust system

For 2019, the R1250GS and the new BMW R1250RT feature Automobiles Automatic Stability Control (ASC) and Hill Start Control for assistance starting on inclines. In addition, the optional Riding Modes Pro feature adds Dynamic Riding Mode, Dynamic Traction Control (DTC) and, in the R1250GS, Dynamic Pro, Enduro and Enduro Pro riding modes

The hardware of the DTC and Dynamic Braking Assistant (DBC) is not specified, but BMW claims that the DTC allows even safer acceleration when cornering, while the DBC provides safer deceleration by avoiding unintended acceleration and controlling torque during braking.

For the R1250RT, BMW has added its Dynamic ESA electronic suspension with fully automatic load compensation

The new R1250GS now has equipment connectivity as standard. This includes a 6.5-inch full-colour TFT screen and it works with BMW motorcyclesrrad's Multi-Controller for access to motorcycling and connectivity functions. The R1250RT features a large 5.7-inch TFT colour screen, which is complemented by an analogue speedometer and rev counter.

Finally, rounding out the changes are a standard LED headlight for the R1250GS, optional LED daytime riding lights for both models, an optional Intelligent Emergency Call that can automatically notify emergency services.
