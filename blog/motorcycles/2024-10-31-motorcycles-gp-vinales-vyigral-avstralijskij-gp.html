MAVERICK Vinales ended Yamaha's record-breaking motoGP victory with an emphatic win at the Phillip Island Grand Prix of Australia

The Spaniard ran home a clear winner after jumping in front in the early laps, helped by a shocking incident delivered by both Marc Marquez and Johann Zarco.

Andrea Iannone and Andrea Dovizioso made the podium and Jack Miller came in seventh, celebrating at Parker Farm as the team's first independent rider.

The win is the first for Yamaha since the 2017 Dutch TT and ends a losing streak to Vinales that stretched to 28 races.

Like last year, Miller again led the first lap of his main grand prix, but this time it wasn't easy.

It was his Pramac teammate Danilo Petrucci who led into the South Loop, only to have a big moment that sent him skating into the gravel.

Polesitter Marquez was another who got wide in the early corners, but left to be right on Miller's tail to work his way into the lead late in the first round
