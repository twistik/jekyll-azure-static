Technical details and specs for the upcoming 2019 BMW S 1000 RR have been leaked online, and if these specs are genuine, it seems the updated BMW S 1000 RR will get more technology and make more power than the outgoing model. For starters, the BMW S 1000 RR gets a new engine - a 999cc, inline four-cylinder, liquid-cooled engine with what BMW calls ShiftCam variable valve technology. Rated power, according to leaks, is 207bhp at 13,500rpm and maximum torque of 113Nm at 11,000rpm. ShiftCam technology is essentially BMW motorcyclesrrad's unique variable valve technology, which made its debut on the new BMW R 1250 GS earlier this year.

Curb weight (fully fuelled) of the 2019 BMW S 1000 RR is specified as 197kg, while the optional M package with carbon fibre components will have a curb weight of 193.5kg. Standard equipment includes four riding modes (‘Rain’, ‘Road’, ‘Dynamic’ and ‘Race’), Dynamic Traction Control (DTC), Hill Start Control (HSC) and Shift Assist Pro. Also new full-colour TFT display

2019 BMW S 1000 RR will be unveiled at the upcoming EICMA show in Milan, Italy on 5 November 2024. BMW motorcyclesrrad has already released the design of the new motorcycle and visually there are several changes in the design. The most obvious changes in the new model are the headlights.
